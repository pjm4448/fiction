## SCENE 1: THE AUDITION

The room was a lie, polished to a sterile perfection that screamed of the filth it was designed to conceal. It smelled of recycled air and expensive, synthesized wood, a scent that failed to mask the faint, underlying tang of antiseptic and something metallic, like old blood. Gideon Cross, a man whose tailored suit was a more effective form of armor than anything the grunts in the lower city wore, gestured to the single, brutally elegant chair opposite his desk.

Her clothes were a patchwork of scavenged tech and worn leather, her hair a chaotic spill of black, and her eyes... her eyes were old, filled with the weary, patient hatred of the perpetually hunted. She scanned the room, not for exits (she was too much of a realist for that) but for the things that could kill her. The almost invisible seams of the automated turrets in the ceiling, the subtle shimmer in the air that betrayed a laser grid, the pressure plates she could feel even through the soles of her worn boots.

Cross's smile was a thin, bloodless wound. "An impressive threat assessment. A shame such talents are wasted on... freelance acquisitions."

"I get by," Nyx's voice was a low, gravelly thing, the sound of rocks grinding together in the dark.

"You get caught," Cross corrected, his voice still infuriatingly calm. He steepled his fingers, the picture of corporate serenity. "That little job at the Kestrel data-haven. Messy. Very loud. He slid a data-slate across the desk. It shimmered to life, displaying a contract so dense with legalese it was a weapon in itself. Nyx didn't look at it. She looked at Cross, at the predator behind the expensive suit, at the void behind his eyes.

"What's the job?" she asked.

"It's not a job. It's a project," Cross said. "A chance to be a part of something... transcendent. We have a researcher, a man of singular, almost divine, talent. Dr. Kaito Soren. He requires a subject for a new initiative. Something that will redefine the very nature of consciousness."

"A lab-rat," Nyx spat, the word an insult.

"A muse," Cross countered, his smile widening, showing a hint of teeth. "He is an artist, and he requires a canvas of exceptional quality. Resilient. Defiant. Someone who will not... break... under the intensity of his vision. We have tried with others. They were... disappointing."

The unspoken words hung in the air, thick and cloying. They broke. They died. They screamed until their minds dissolved.

Nyx felt a cold dread, a familiar companion, but this time it was different. It was laced with a strange, perverse curiosity. She had spent her life fighting, surviving, being the monster in the dark. What kind of monster would she become in the hands of a man who saw the breaking of a human being as an act of creation?

"And what do I get?" she asked, her voice a low whisper.

"Everything," Cross said, his voice a silken promise. "A new life. A purpose. A chance to be more than just a survivor. A chance to be a work of art."

He leaned forward, his eyes locking onto hers, and, for the first time, she saw it. Not just the predator, but the fanatic. The true believer.

"So, Nyx," he said, the name a caress and a threat. "Will you be our masterpiece?"

As Nyx considered the offer, a fragment of her past flickered unbidden: a scavenged data-port hack in the undercity shadows, where she'd interfaced her body with a corporate terminal, the neural stims flooding her with reluctant, shuddering arousal amid the data theft. Her defiance had always been laced with such violations, her flesh a tool in the endless grind. But this... this promised something darker, more invasive.

## SCENE 3: THE FIRST SIGN OF RESISTANCE

Soren began to laugh. A low, breathless, ecstatic sound. He got to his feet, wiping the blood from his lip with the back of his hand. His creation was so much more than a canvas - It was a collaborator and a worthy opponent.

## SCENE 5: A LOVER'S QUARREL
